# HugoMojo Artisan AI Income Path & Niche Action System

## 1. Your Match

You matched as an Artisan.

The Artisan path is for people who are better at packaging knowledge, taste, creative judgment, or niche experience into a sellable digital asset.

This path is not about chasing clients every day. It is about building a useful product once, testing demand, then improving the asset over time.

## 2. Why You Got This Result

Artisan matches usually appear when the scan detects several of these signals:

- You have a topic, skill, taste, or experience base that can be packaged.
- You prefer assets over constant client communication.
- You may already have some audience, expertise, or creative judgment.
- You are willing to build before the first sale.
- You are more patient than a Hunter, but you still need a focused first product.
- You can use AI to speed up writing, structuring, design, and packaging.

The core diagnosis: your fastest path is not doing random AI tasks. It is turning what you already know into a small paid asset.

## 3. Market Fit Model

The Artisan sits in the knowledge and asset gap.

People buy digital products when they want:

- A shortcut
- A template
- A curated system
- A specific answer
- A faster way to complete a task

The market does not need another broad ebook. It needs a specific product that solves a specific problem for a specific person.

Good Artisan products are usually small:

- A prompt pack
- A checklist
- A guide
- A Notion template
- A spreadsheet calculator
- A swipe file
- A mini SOP
- A Canva template pack

The first product should be useful before it is beautiful.

## 4. Niche Direction

Start with a niche where the problem is repeated, the buyer understands the value quickly, and AI can help you deliver faster.

Best starter niche directions:

1. Job-specific templates or checklists
2. Niche creator or seller asset packs
3. Small how-to guides for repeated problems

For Artisan, the best niche is a repeated problem that can become a small asset, template, checklist, guide, or digital product.

## 5. Niche Filter

Before choosing a niche, run it through this filter:

1. Can you reach 20 people in this niche?
2. Do they repeat this problem often?
3. Can AI help you deliver faster?
4. Can you create a small first offer in 7 days?
5. Would this buyer understand the value quickly?

If the answer is no to most of these, do not start there yet. Pick a narrower niche.

## 6. Best First Product

Start with one of these formats:

### Option A: Prompt Pack

Best if your topic involves repeatable thinking, writing, planning, or decision-making.

Example:

"20 prompts for solo consultants to turn messy client notes into clear proposals."

### Option B: Practical Guide

Best if you can explain a process clearly.

Example:

"A 7-day guide for first-time Etsy sellers to create and list their first printable product."

### Option C: Template Or Checklist

Best if your audience repeats the same task often.

Example:

"Client onboarding checklist for AI freelancers."

### Option D: Niche Asset Pack

Best if your taste or visual judgment is strong.

Example:

"AI-generated product image prompt pack for handmade jewelry sellers."

Pick a product that can be built in 7 days. If it takes longer, the scope is too large.

## 7. Tool Stack

Use a practical creation stack:

- Claude or ChatGPT: outline, drafting, prompt creation, product copy.
- Canva: PDF layout, cover, product mockups, simple templates.
- Google Docs or Notion: product drafting and internal structure.
- Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, or Creem: simple product checkout.
- X, Reddit, LinkedIn, Etsy, or niche communities: first feedback channel.

Do not build a website first. Build the asset and one clear product page.

## 8. 30-Day Action Plan

### Week 1: Find The Product Problem

- Pick one audience.
- List 10 annoying tasks they repeat.
- Choose one task that can be improved with a template, guide, or prompt pack.
- Write the product promise in one sentence.

### Week 2: Build Version 1

- Draft the outline.
- Use AI to create the first version.
- Add examples and instructions.
- Package it as a simple PDF, Notion page, or template.

### Week 3: Publish And Test

- Create one product page.
- Share it in one channel.
- Ask for feedback from 10 relevant people.
- Track clicks, replies, saves, and objections.

### Week 4: Improve The Asset

- Fix unclear sections.
- Add one missing example.
- Improve the title and product promise.
- Decide whether to keep, repackage, or start a new asset.

## 9. Copy-Paste Prompts And Templates

### Product Idea Prompt

```text
Act as a digital product strategist.

I know about:
[TOPIC]

My likely audience:
[AUDIENCE]

Generate 10 small digital product ideas.

For each idea include:
1. Product title
2. Specific audience
3. Painful problem solved
4. Product format
5. What is included
6. Price range
7. First place to test it
```

### Product Page Prompt

```text
Create a simple product page for this digital product:

Product:
[PRODUCT]

Audience:
[AUDIENCE]

Problem:
[PROBLEM]

Include:
- headline
- one-sentence promise
- what is inside
- who it is for
- who it is not for
- simple price justification
- refund/access note
- short CTA
```

### Feedback Request Template

```text
Hi [NAME] - I made a small resource for [AUDIENCE] who struggle with [PROBLEM].

It is a [FORMAT] that helps them [OUTCOME].

Could I send you the page and ask one question: does the promise feel useful, or is it too vague?
```

## 10. Resource Gap Warnings

Watch these failure points:

- Product too broad. A product for everyone is usually bought by no one.
- Building for months without feedback.
- Over-designing before proving demand.
- Pricing based on effort instead of usefulness.
- Avoiding distribution. Products do not sell just because they exist.

## 11. Your Next Step Today

Write this sentence:

"I am building a [format] for [audience] who need help with [specific problem]."

Then ask 10 people if that problem is real. The Artisan path starts with a useful asset, not a perfect brand.
