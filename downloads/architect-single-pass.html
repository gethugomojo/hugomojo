Architect AI Income Path & Niche Action System

A focused action system for turning your current resources into a clear first market test.

THE ARCHITECT

Diagnosing workflows, translating AI tools into business outcomes, and making operational value visible.

01 · YOUR MATCH

You matched as an Architect.

PATH LOGIC

The Architect path is for people who can see systems, diagnose workflow problems, and translate AI tools into business outcomes.

CORE DIAGNOSIS

Your strongest path is not selling generic AI content. It is finding operational pain and turning it into a paid audit, setup, or automation project.

02 · TODAY'S PRIORITY
List 20 businesses with one repeated workflow. Not code. Diagnosis first.

The Architect path starts with seeing the workflow before selling the tool.

03 · SIGNALS DETECTED
✓You are comfortable thinking in workflows and systems.
✓You can communicate with clients or business owners.
✓You are more patient with diagnosis than a pure Hunter.
✓You may not need a large audience because the customer value is higher.
✓You can use AI, no-code tools, or automation platforms to solve repeated problems.
✓You are interested in higher-value work rather than many small transactions.
04 · MARKET FIT MODEL
OPERATIONS

Lead intake, email follow-up, report writing, CRM updates, and client onboarding.

KNOWLEDGE

Internal knowledge search, proposal generation, customer support drafts, and SOPs.

VALUE

The market pays more when the problem is tied to time saved, revenue protected, or errors reduced.

The Architect wins by diagnosing the workflow before selling the tool.

05 · NICHE DIRECTION

Start with a niche where the problem is repeated, the buyer understands the value quickly, and AI can help you deliver faster.

DIRECTION 1

Service business intake and follow-up workflows.

DIRECTION 2

Agency reporting or client operations workflows.

DIRECTION 3

Small business SOP, CRM, or admin workflows.

For Architect, the best niche is a business process that repeats often, creates friction, and can be improved with AI-assisted systems.

06 · NICHE FILTER

Before choosing a niche, run it through this filter:

✓Can you reach 20 people in this niche?
✓Do they repeat this problem often?
✓Can AI help you deliver faster?
✓Can you create a small first offer in 7 days?
✓Would this buyer understand the value quickly?

If the answer is no to most of these, do not start there yet. Pick a narrower niche.

07 · YOUR FIRST OFFER
OFFER NAME
AI Workflow Audit
DELIVERABLES
  • Workflow map and bottleneck list.
  • AI automation opportunities and tool stack recommendation.
  • A 30-day implementation plan for one business workflow.
SUGGESTED STARTER PRICING

$150-$500 for the first audit, depending on scope and buyer. This is a starting reference, not an income promise.

08 · OFFER OPTIONS
OPTION A

Workflow Audit

"I review one repetitive workflow in your business and show where AI or automation can save time."

OPTION B

Assistant Setup

"I help you create a focused internal AI assistant for one business process."

OPTION C

Automation Pilot

"I automate one admin process using AI and no-code tools, then document the workflow."

Start with an audit if you are not sure what to build. Audits are easier to sell than unknown implementation projects.

09 · TOOL STACK
DIAGNOSE
  • ChatGPT or Claude for workflow analysis, SOP drafting, and assistant instructions.
  • Notion or Google Docs for SOPs and internal documentation.
  • Loom for short walkthrough videos for clients.
IMPLEMENT
  • Make or Zapier for automation between apps.
  • Google Sheets or Airtable for a lightweight operating database.
  • Calendly or Tally for simple intake and discovery forms.
10 · 7-DAY SPRINT
□ Day 1 · Choose one business niche.
□ Day 2 · List 10 repetitive workflows they likely have.
□ Day 3 · Choose one workflow and write an audit offer.
□ Day 4 · Build a 10-question intake form.
□ Day 5 · Prepare a one-page audit report template.
□ Day 6 · Send 10 targeted audit messages.
□ Day 7 · Send 10 more and book one diagnostic conversation.
11 · 30-DAY ACTION PLAN
WEEK 1

Pick a business niche, list 10 repetitive workflows, choose the workflow with the clearest time cost, and write one audit offer.

WEEK 2

Create the audit system: intake form, workflow map template, one-page audit report, and diagnostic call structure.

WEEK 3

Send audit outreach: focus on one pain point, offer a small diagnostic, and track which workflow pains get replies.

WEEK 4

Convert to a pilot: define inputs, outputs, tools, timeline, and package the final workflow as a repeatable offer.

✓Talking about AI instead of business pain.
✓Trying to build before diagnosing.
✓Choosing huge workflows for the first project.
✓Forgetting documentation. Businesses pay more when the workflow can be repeated.
